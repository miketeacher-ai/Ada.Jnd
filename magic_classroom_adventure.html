Grammar Castle

Usually or now? The castle doors know!

🔵 Present Simple: usual life

We use Present Simple for things we do every day, usually, sometimes, always, and for facts.

I / You / We / They + base verb
I read. They play.

He / She / It + verb-s/-es
She reads. The cat sleeps.

Negative: I don’t eat onions. He doesn’t eat onions.

Question: Do you like bananas? Does she like bananas?

🩷 Present Continuous: happening now

We use Present Continuous for actions happening now, right now, at the moment, or temporarily.

I am + verb-ing
I am reading.

You / We / They are + verb-ing
They are playing.

He / She / It is + verb-ing
The dog is running.

Negative: She isn’t watching TV.

Question: Is the monkey jumping?

👾 Grammar Monster Mistakes

Look carefully! With does/doesn’t, the next verb loses its s. Actions now need am/is/are + verb-ing.

❌ He go to school every day. → ✅ He goes to school every day.
❌ She is eat an apple. → ✅ She is eating an apple.
❌ They is playing football. → ✅ They are playing football.
❌ Does he likes pizza? → ✅ Does he like pizza?
❌ The cat sleeping now. → ✅ The cat is sleeping now.
❌ My father cook dinner on Sundays. → ✅ My father cooks dinner on Sundays.
❌ I am play with my dog. → ✅ I am playing with my dog.
❌ She don’t like carrots. → ✅ She doesn’t like carrots.
❌ She like apples. → ✅ She likes apples.
❌ My brother watch TV. → ✅ My brother watches TV.

Part 1: The Magic Bag

Mia studies English at Sunny School every day. She usually carries a blue schoolbag 🎒, writes with a yellow pencil ✏️, and eats an apple 🍎 at break time. Her brother Leo often walks with her. Their mother puts a healthy sandwich 🥪 in Mia’s lunchbox.

This morning, Mia opens the classroom door and sees a small purple bag under her desk. “That is not my bag,” she says. The bag is shaking! A quiet bell is ringing inside it.

Mia is opening the magic bag now. A cute rabbit 🐰 is eating a carrot 🥕 beside a ruler. A funny monkey 🐵 is holding a pencil and is drawing a huge banana. A red apple is dancing on a notebook, and three green peas are playing football with an eraser.

Then a tiny teacher 🧑‍🏫 climbs out. She usually teaches animals in Magic Town, but today she is writing on a long banana board. “Welcome to the Magic Classroom!” she cries. “Our clock sings every hour, our computer likes soup, and our chairs sometimes dance.”

Leo is looking through the window now. A brave dog 🐶 is reading a book, a sleepy cat 🐱 is sitting on the teacher’s hat, and a colorful bird 🐦 is drinking orange juice. Mia’s ordinary classroom is changing. The posters are smiling, the scissors are clapping, and the lunchbox is singing.

Mia usually feels quiet in class, but now she is laughing. She gives the rabbit some lettuce and asks, “Does this happen every day?” The tiny teacher smiles. “No. Magic usually sleeps on Mondays. Today is Tuesday!”

On the shelf, a little fish is swimming inside a glass pencil case. A yellow duck is washing a plate, and a panda is making tomato soup. The room smells delicious. Mia usually likes quiet lessons, but this noisy lesson feels exciting.

Mini glossary: shaking = moving quickly · tiny = very small · ordinary = normal · break time = rest time at school · lettuce = a green vegetable

Part 2: The Crazy Classroom

Look at the classroom now! Everything is moving. The cat 🐱 is sleeping on the teacher’s desk, but its tail is drawing circles on a worksheet. The brave dog 🐶 is reading a blue book aloud. It is wearing Mia’s glasses.

Near the window, the magic pencil is jumping over a ruler. Two apples 🍎 are singing a noisy song, and a banana is dancing with the classroom clock. The students are laughing, but the tiny teacher is trying to start the lesson.

“Please sit down!” she says. Nobody is sitting. The rabbit 🐰 is climbing the bookshelf. The monkey 🐵 is writing “I LOVE PIZZA” on the board. A turtle 🐢 is carrying a lunchbox, and three mice are eating cheese inside it.

Mia is helping the teacher. She is closing the window and Leo is collecting the flying papers. Suddenly, the computer is showing a picture of a giant strawberry. The strawberry is waving and saying, “Listen! The Grammar Monster is coming!”

Everyone is becoming quiet. A green monster is walking through the door. It looks scary, but it is holding a tiny spoon. “I am not eating students,” it says. “I am looking for my yogurt.” The class finds the yogurt under the panda’s chair. The monster is smiling now, and all the children are cheering.

Under the map, an elephant is holding four crayons. It is coloring a small butterfly while a fox is cutting purple paper. A clever bee is counting the markers. Even the bin is opening and closing its lid.

Part 3: Every Day vs Today

Every day, Mia wakes up at seven and eats cereal for breakfast. Today, she is eating pizza in the classroom. She usually drinks milk in the morning, but now she is drinking purple mango juice.

Every morning, the teacher writes on the board. Now, the monkey is writing on it, and the teacher is dancing with a broom. The students usually sit on chairs. At the moment, they are sitting on soft clouds above their desks.

The rabbit usually eats carrots. Today, it is eating a cheese sandwich. The cat often sleeps under the desk. Right now, it is riding a little bicycle. The dog normally runs in the garden, but it is reading a story to a sleepy lion.

Leo plays football after school. Now, he is playing football with an orange and two potatoes. Mia’s mother usually cooks soup on Tuesdays. Today, she is making pancakes for the tiny teacher. Mia’s father often washes the plates, but now he is holding a singing plate and laughing.

At the end of a normal school day, Mia does her homework and goes to bed at nine. Tonight is different. The magic animals are visiting her family, the apples are sleeping in her schoolbag, and the Grammar Monster is telling a funny bedtime story. Mia smiles. Routines are useful, but magical days are wonderful too!

On normal days, the school clock shows the time and the calendar hangs quietly. Today, the clock is singing and the calendar is flying around the room. The children usually open their books, but now their books are telling jokes.

Teacher Zone

Planning, answers, differentiation, and correction support.

Lesson profile

Aims: distinguish routines from actions now; form third-person verbs; use am/is/are + -ing; speak and write short connected texts.

Level: A1–A2 · Age: 7–12

Vocabulary: classroom, home, animals, food, family, adjectives, routines, actions.

Materials: this offline file, screen or tablets, pencils, printed worksheet.

Core procedure

Warm up with mime. Contrast “I usually…” with “I am … now.” Read one story section. Check meaning before form. Move into controlled games, then pair speaking and independent writing. Finish with reflection.

60-minute plan
5 warm-up · 10 grammar · 10 vocabulary · 10 story · 15 games · 10 speaking/writing
75-minute plan
8 warm-up · 12 grammar · 12 vocabulary · 13 story · 18 games · 7 speaking · 5 writing/reflection
90-minute plan
10 warm-up · 15 grammar · 15 vocabulary · 15 story · 20 games · 10 speaking · 5 reflection/homework
Warm-up and controlled practice
Mime five routines. Children say “You usually…” Then freeze an action and ask “What am I doing now?” During controlled practice, ask learners to circle time clues before choosing a tense.
Speaking and writing practice
Model one full answer, rehearse in pairs, then change partners. For writing, color-code routine verbs blue and now phrases pink. Use the checklist before teacher correction.
Support and challenge
Support: reduce choices, keep sentence starters visible, pair with a patient model, allow oral rehearsal. Challenge: add negatives, questions, frequency adverbs, conjunctions, and a surprising final sentence.
Common mistakes and correction
Use fingers to show the three parts of is + verb + ing. Tap the final s for he/she/it. After does/doesn’t, sweep the extra s away. Recast first, then ask the child to self-correct.
Homework and extensions
Assign the printable homework below. Extension: create a six-panel routine/now comic, interview a family member, or write a new Magic Classroom chapter.
